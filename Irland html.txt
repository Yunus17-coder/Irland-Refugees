  <section>
    <h2>Why Did Irish Refugees Escape?</h2>
    <p>The Irish Potato Famine (1845–1852) caused massive starvation and poverty in Ireland. Over a million people died, and another million fled the country. They sought safety and opportunity in places like the United States, Canada, and Britain.</p>
  </section>

  <section>
    <h2>How Were Irish Refugees Treated?</h2>
    <ul>
      <li>Many faced discrimination for being Catholic and poor.</li>
      <li>In the U.S., signs often said "No Irish Need Apply".</li>
      <li>They lived in overcrowded, unsanitary neighborhoods.</li>
      <li>Despite the hardship, many Irish refugees contributed greatly to the societies they joined.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <section>
    <h2>Fun Facts About Irish Immigration</h2>
    <ul>
      <li>Today, over 30 million Americans claim Irish ancestry.</li>
      <li>St. Patrick’s Day is widely celebrated in countries with Irish immigrants.</li>
      <li>Some of the earliest labor unions in the U.S. were formed by Irish workers.</li>
    </ul>
